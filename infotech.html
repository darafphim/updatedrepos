OVERVIEW

The implementation of technology to manage and process data is the focus of the area of Information Technology (IT). Computer-based information systems' design, development, installation, and management are all incorporated. IT specialists work in a range of sectors, from financial services to healthcare, to increase productivity and efficiency by utilizing technology.

IT is frequently given in academic programs as an areas of expertise within a wider computer science or business major. Typically, students majoring in IT develop a wide range of abilities, including project management, networking, programming, database management, and cybersecurity. Additionally, they will gain knowledge about how technology may assist corporate operations and be used to address real-world issues.

REQUIREMENTS

To pursue a college course in Information Technology in the Philippines, students need to satisfy specific requirements that may vary based on the school or university. One of the fundamental prerequisites is the completion of high school education or its equivalent, which is a high school diploma. Additionally, some schools may require students to take a college entrance exam, which evaluates their knowledge, aptitude, and readiness for higher education.

Apart from these general requirements, specific schools or programs may have additional prerequisites. For instance, certain institutions may require students to have a minimum grade point average (GPA) to be considered for admission. Moreover, some schools may require applicants to have completed specific subjects in high school, such as mathematics, science, and computer-related courses, to be eligible for an IT course. Furthermore, transcripts are an essential part of the admission process, and most schools will consider a student's academic performance and achievements in high school. Therefore, students may need to provide their academic records, including grades, awards, and other relevant information to be evaluated for admission.
